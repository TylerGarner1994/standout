# Copywriting Standards

## Voice & Tone
- Write like a human, not a corporation
- Conversational but credible
- Specific over vague — numbers, timeframes, percentages
- Active voice always — passive voice is the enemy of conversion
- No jargon unless the audience speaks it

## Headlines
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Use specificity: "40-60 points" not "dramatically improve"
- Create curiosity gaps that demand reading more
- Test the "bar stool test" — would someone say this to a friend?
- Maximum 15 words for H1, 12 words for H2

## CTAs
- Action-oriented: "Get", "Start", "Book", "Claim", "Download"
- First person when possible: "Start My Free Trial" > "Start Your Free Trial"
- No trailing periods on CTA text
- No weak CTAs: "Submit", "Click Here", "Learn More" (unless strategic)
- Include value reinforcement: "Get My Free Guide" > "Download"

## Body Copy
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bucket brigades to maintain momentum ("Here's the thing...", "But that's not all...", "Here's why this matters...")
- One idea per paragraph
- Scannable — use subheads, bullets, bold key phrases
- Write at 8th grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)

## Forbidden Patterns
- ❌ Em dashes (—) — rewrite or use en dash (–)
- ❌ "Unlock your potential" — too vague
- ❌ "In today's fast-paced world" — cliché opener
- ❌ "Look no further" — overused
- ❌ "Are you tired of..." — weak opening
- ❌ "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" — unsubstantiated superlatives
- ❌ Fake testimonial names with ages ("Sarah, 43") — use "Verified Buyer" or real names
- ❌ Stock photo descriptions in testimonials

## Advertorial-Specific
- Publication-style framing (looks like editorial content)
- Sponsored content disclaimer in footer (REQUIRED)
- Third-person narrative or journalistic voice
- Case study / discovery story format
- Build credibility through specificity, not hype

## Social Proof Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)
1. Specific customer results with names/details
2. Aggregate data ("10,000+ customers", "4.9/5 from 965 reviews")
3. Media logos ("As seen in...")
4. Expert endorsements
5. Industry certifications / awards
6. Generic testimonials

## Persuasion Sequence (Page Flow)
1. **Pattern interrupt** — Stop the scroll with an unexpected hook
2. **Problem agitation** — Name their pain specifically
3. **Failed solutions** — Show why what they've tried doesn't work
4. **Discovery story** — Introduce the solution through narrative
5. **Mechanism** — Explain HOW it works (builds credibility)
6. **Proof** — Testimonials, data, case studies
7. **Offer** — What they get, priced against value
8. **Risk reversal** — Guarantee, free trial, money back
9. **Urgency** — Why act now, not later
10. **CTA** — Clear, compelling, friction-free
