The Connection Her Doctor Never Mentioned

Sarah's sister is a nutritionist in Portland. During a Sunday phone call, Sarah broke down crying about the night sweats. Her sister said five words that changed the trajectory of Sarah's menopause: "It's probably your gut."

She explained something that most OB-GYNs still don't discuss with patients: the gut-hormone axis. Your gut microbiome doesn't just digest food. It produces and regulates 95% of your body's serotonin and directly controls how your body metabolises estrogen through a collection of bacteria called the estrobolome.

When your gut bacteria fall out of balance, and they almost always do during perimenopause, your body can't properly process the estrogen fluctuations that cause hot flashes, night sweats, mood swings, and brain fog. You're not just experiencing hormone changes. You're experiencing hormone changes without the bacterial support system designed to manage them.
