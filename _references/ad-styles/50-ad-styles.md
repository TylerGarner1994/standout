# 50 AD STYLE PROMPTS

This document contains 50 expert-level ad style prompts for the unicorn-ad-creative-engine skill.
Each prompt defines a complete creative approach including visual style, copy structure, and psychological mechanism.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS REFERENCE

Each ad style includes:
- **Style Name** - The approach identifier
- **Best For** - Awareness stage and audience type
- **Visual Direction** - What it looks like
- **Copy Structure** - How the words are arranged
- **Psychology** - Why it works
- **Example Hook** - Sample opening line

---

## STYLES 1-10: PROOF-BASED STYLES

### Style 1: The Screenshotted Result

**Best For:** Warm traffic, product-aware audiences

**Visual Direction:**
A screenshot of an actual result (dashboard, metric, review) slightly cropped so the most impressive number is visible but context requires reading

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "This came in this morning..."
Body: [Screenshot image]
Caption: [What the result means + how to get it]
CTA: [Specific next step]
```

**Psychology:** Specificity trigger + social proof + curiosity (what's the context?)

**Example Hook:** "This came in from a client we've been working with for 90 days..."

---

### Style 2: The Before/After Split

**Best For:** All traffic temperatures for visual products/services

**Visual Direction:**
Split screen, left labeled "Before [timeframe]" right labeled "After [timeframe]". Both sides show same metric/situation with obvious improvement.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Timeframe] ago vs. today."
Body: [Split image with labeled metrics]
Caption: Here's the exact process that created this change:
[3 steps]
CTA: Want these results?
```

**Psychology:** Transformation trigger + proof + aspiration

**Example Hook:** "90 days ago vs. today. Same ad account. Very different results."

---

### Style 3: The Third-Party Validation

**Best For:** Trust-building for skeptical audiences

**Visual Direction:**
Screenshot of review, publication mention, or award. Must look authentic - no overly designed graphics around it.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: [Quote from third party in large text]
Body: [Source attribution]
Caption: [What this means for the prospect + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Authority bias + social proof + credibility transfer

**Example Hook:** "'The only agency we've seen consistently deliver 3x+ ROAS for e-commerce.' - [Publication]"

---

### Style 4: The Live Dashboard

**Best For:** B2B, marketing services, performance-based offers

**Visual Direction:**
Actual platform dashboard (Meta Ads Manager, Google Analytics, etc.) showing positive metrics. Can be video or screenshot.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Real account. Real numbers. No editing."
Body: [Dashboard visual]
Caption: [What the numbers mean + how they were achieved]
CTA: [Book audit / See how]
```

**Psychology:** Specificity trigger + authenticity + proof

**Example Hook:** "Pulled this from a client account this morning. $47,293 in revenue on $12,840 ad spend. Here's how."

---

### Style 5: The Testimonial Stack

**Best For:** Consideration stage, overcoming skepticism

**Visual Direction:**
Multiple testimonials (3-5) shown rapidly in video or stacked in static. Real names, real results. Variety of people.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Don't take our word for it."
Body: [Multiple testimonial screenshots or video clips]
Caption: [What they all have in common + what you do differently]
CTA: [Join them]
```

**Psychology:** Social proof cascade + bandwagon + credibility

**Example Hook:** "We asked 5 clients to describe their experience. Here's what they said."

---

### Style 6: The Case Study Reveal

**Best For:** High-consideration B2B, consultative sales

**Visual Direction:**
Timeline or journey visual showing month-by-month progression. Graph line going up preferred.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Client type] was at [before state]. 90 days later..."
Body: [Journey visualization]
Caption: [The 3 specific things that created this result]
CTA: [Want the same analysis for your business?]
```

**Psychology:** Narrative structure + aspiration + specificity

**Example Hook:** "E-commerce brand was at 1.4x ROAS and had tried 2 previous agencies. Here's what we found in week 1."

---

### Style 7: The Comparison Table

**Best For:** Solution-aware audiences choosing between options

**Visual Direction:**
Clean comparison table: Your solution vs. "Typical Agency" or "Doing It Yourself". Checkmarks and X marks. Simple, readable.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Not all [solutions] are equal."
Body: [Comparison table visual]
Caption: [What the table can't show: your specific advantage]
CTA: [See if we're the right fit]
```

**Psychology:** Differentiation + authority + decision simplification

**Example Hook:** "Here's what separates a unicorn agency from a typical one."

---

### Style 8: The Number Drop

**Best For:** Cold to warm traffic, performance services

**Visual Direction:**
Large, bold number as the dominant visual element. Everything else supports it. High contrast background.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Visual: [$47,293] or [3.4x] in massive typography
Hook: "This is what [timeframe/effort] looks like."
Caption: [What it took to get there + how to replicate]
CTA: [Start the process]
```

**Psychology:** Pattern interrupt + anchoring + curiosity

**Example Hook:** "$47,293. That's what 90 days of systematic creative testing generated for one account."

---

### Style 9: The Video Testimonial

**Best For:** All stages, highest trust-building format

**Visual Direction:**
Authentic customer on camera. Phone quality preferred over studio. Real environment (home office, business). Genuine emotion.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Video: [Customer tells their story naturally]
Caption: "[Pull quote from video] - [Name, Business Type]"
CTA: [Watch the full story / Book similar results]
```

**Psychology:** Face trigger + authenticity + social proof

**Example Hook:** "I was skeptical. I'd been burned before. But here's what happened in the first 30 days..."

---

### Style 10: The Audit Reveal

**Best For:** B2B, marketing services, cold traffic with problem awareness

**Visual Direction:**
Screen recording or screenshot of an actual account audit. Annotations showing problems found. Feels like getting a sneak peek.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "We analyzed [X] ad accounts and found the same 3 problems in [Y%] of them."
Body: [Audit visual with annotations]
Caption: [What these problems cost + how to fix]
CTA: [Get your account audited]
```

**Psychology:** Authority + loss aversion + reciprocity (free value preview)

**Example Hook:** "We've now audited 500+ Meta ad accounts. Here's the #1 mistake we see every time."

## STYLES 11-20: HOOK-FORWARD STYLES

### Style 11: The Bold Claim

**Best For:** Cold traffic with high skepticism markets

**Visual Direction:**
Minimal. Strong typography on clean background. The claim IS the visual.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: [Audacious, specific, believable claim]
Body: [3 reasons why this is true]
Caption: [Proof + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Pattern interrupt + curiosity + authority

**Example Hook:** "We've never lost a client due to results. Here's why that's not luck."

---

### Style 12: The Controversial Take

**Best For:** Thought leadership, cold traffic engagement

**Visual Direction:**
Face-forward video or text card with opinion statement. Designed to create reaction.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Unpopular opinion: [contrarian view of common practice]"
Body: [Why the conventional wisdom is wrong]
Caption: [What works instead + evidence]
CTA: [Discuss / Learn more]
```

**Psychology:** Confirmation bias disruption + curiosity + authority positioning

**Example Hook:** "Unpopular opinion: Your creative isn't the problem with your Meta ads."

---

### Style 13: The Mistake Reveal

**Best For:** Problem-aware cold traffic

**Visual Direction:**
Person looking at screen with concerning expression, or split-screen of mistake vs. correct approach

```
Hook: "The [X] mistake [target audience] makes that costs [$Y] per month"

Caption: [How to fix it]

### Style 14: The Secret Reveal

**Best For:** Cold to warm traffic

**Visual Direction:**
Closed/locked visual opening up, or person looking over shoulder conspiratorially. Implies insider knowledge.

**Copy Structure:**
```

Caption: [Why this matters to them + CTA]

**Example Hook:** "What Meta's algorithm actually rewards (it's not what their reps tell you)."

---

### Style 15: The Myth Buster

**Best For:** Educated audiences with incorrect assumptions

**Visual Direction:**
"MYTH" vs "REALITY" visual structure. Clear contradiction of common belief.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Myth: [Common false belief] Reality: [Corrected truth]"
Body: [Why the myth exists + why it's wrong]
Caption: [What to do instead]
CTA: [Learn the right approach]

**Psychology:** Confirmation bias disruption + authority + education

---

### Style 16: The Warning

**Best For:** Problem-aware audiences, high-stakes decisions

**Visual Direction:**
Warning symbol, caution tape, or serious face. Creates urgency through concern.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Warning: If you're [doing X], this is costing you [specific amount]"
Body: [Why this is a problem + what happens if uncorrected]
Caption: [The fix + urgency to act now]
CTA: [Stop the bleeding]
```

**Psychology:** Loss aversion + urgency + authority

**Example Hook:** "Warning: If you're using broad targeting without creative segmentation, you're leaving 40-60% of your ROAS on the table."

---

### Style 17: The Question Hook

**Best For:** Universal - works across all temperatures

**Visual Direction:**
Large question text or face looking directly at camera asking the question

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Question that identifies their exact problem or desire]"
Body: [If yes, here's why + solution]
Caption: [Proof + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Identification + curiosity + Zeigarnik effect

**Example Hook:** "Still wondering why your ROAS drops every time you scale?"

---

### Style 18: The If/Then Statement

**Best For:** Qualifying specific audience segments

**Visual Direction:**
Conditional structure made visually clear. IF [condition], THEN [result].

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "If you're [specific situation], then [specific promise]"
Body: [Proof that the promise is real for that situation]
Caption: [How to access the result]
CTA: [Specific to their situation]
```

**Psychology:** Specificity + identification + direct relevance

**Example Hook:** "If you're spending $5K+/month on Meta and your ROAS is under 2x, we can fix that in 90 days."

---

### Style 19: The Industry Insider

**Best For:** B2B, sophisticated audiences

**Visual Direction:**
Professional setting, data-heavy visual, industry terminology used correctly signals expertise

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "After [credible timeframe/data point], here's what we know for certain about [industry topic]"
Body: [3 data-backed insights]
Caption: [Implications for their business + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Authority + specificity + reciprocity (valuable information)

**Example Hook:** "After managing $50M in Meta spend, here's what we know about creative fatigue that changes how you should be testing."

---

### Style 20: The Prediction

**Best For:** Forward-looking audiences, trend-aware markets

**Visual Direction:**
Future-oriented imagery, timeline visual, or confident face making the prediction

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Specific prediction about their market/situation in specific timeframe]"
Body: [Why this will happen + evidence from current trends]
Caption: [What to do now to be on the right side of the prediction]
CTA: [Position yourself now]

**Psychology:** Loss aversion (FOMO) + authority + urgency

**Example Hook:** "Prediction: Brands without a systematic UGC creative process will lose 30-40% of their Meta performance by Q3 2025."

## STYLES 21-30: STORY-BASED STYLES

### Style 21: The Origin Story

**Best For:** Brand awareness, trust building, cold traffic

**Visual Direction:**
Personal, authentic imagery. Founder or real person. Shows the journey visually.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[X years] ago, I was [relatable struggle]"
Body: [What changed + the insight discovered]
Caption: [How this became the product/service + what it means for them]
CTA: [Learn more / Start the journey]
```

**Psychology:** Narrative + empathy + authenticity

**Example Hook:** "5 years ago I was the in-house media buyer watching our agency spend our budget with nothing to show for it."

---

### Style 22: The Customer Journey

**Best For:** Consideration stage, showing the full value

**Visual Direction:**
Timeline or steps visual showing the customer experience from start to result

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Here's what happens when you work with us:"
Body: [Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Result]
Caption: [Each step explained briefly]
CTA: [Start at Step 1]
```

**Psychology:** Expectation setting + risk reduction + clarity

**Example Hook:** "Week 1. Week 4. Week 12. Here's what the journey looks like."

---

### Style 23: The Failure Story

**Best For:** Building empathy with struggling audiences

**Visual Direction:**
Authentic frustrated expression before, satisfied/successful after. Real not staged.

**Copy Structure:**
```

Body: [What went wrong + the turning point]
Caption: [What works instead + how they can apply it]
CTA: [Skip the failure phase]
```

**Psychology:** Empathy + credibility + hope

**Example Hook:** "We lost 3 clients in 6 months before we figured out what actually drives long-term ROAS."

---

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "What [target customer's] Monday morning looks like after working with us:"
Body: [Specific scenes from an improved day]
Caption: [What created this change]
CTA: [Start this journey]
```

**Psychology:** Aspiration + visualization + identification

**Example Hook:** "What this founder's Monday looks like now that their ad account runs itself."

---

---

### Style 26: The Honest Admission

**Visual Direction:**
Direct to camera, minimal production. Authenticity over polish.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "I'll be honest: [admission that lowers guard]"
Body: [The fuller truth that reframes the admission]
Caption: [What this means for them + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Authenticity + confirmation bias (they expected the admission) + trust

**Best For:** Service businesses, consultative sales

Two-part story structure. Part 1 is the struggle. Part 2 is the transformation. Equal visual weight to both.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Part 1: "[Client] came to us [specific struggle in specific terms]"
Part 2: "[Timeframe] later: [specific result in specific terms]"
Caption: [What created the change - briefly]
CTA: [Could this be your story?]
```

**Psychology:** Narrative + social proof + aspiration

### Style 28: The Process Reveal

Step-by-step visual. Flowchart, numbered list, or process diagram. Shows the method clearly.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Our exact [process name] in [X] steps:"

Caption: [Why this process works when others don't]
CTA: [See it applied to your business]

---

### Style 29: The Expert's Take

**Best For:** Established audiences, thought leadership

**Visual Direction:**
Expert on camera or clean typography with attribution. Implies considered opinion.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "After [credibility marker], here's my honest take on [topic]:"
Body: [3-5 specific, opinionated insights]
Caption: [Implications + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Authority + confirmation or disruption of bias + value delivery

**Example Hook:** "After 8 years running paid social for e-commerce brands, here's my honest take on what's working in Meta right now."

---

### Style 30: The Contrast Story

**Best For:** Differentiation from competitors

**Visual Direction:**
Two parallel paths shown visually. Left path = old way / competitor way. Right path = your way.

Body: [What one did vs. what the other did]
Caption: [What made the difference + which path they can choose]
CTA: [Choose the better path]
```

**Psychology:** Choice architecture + differentiation + aspiration

**Example Hook:** "Two e-commerce brands, same ad budget, same year. One grew 340%. One barely survived. Here's the difference."

## STYLES 31-40: DIRECT RESPONSE STYLES

### Style 31: The Direct Offer

**Best For:** Hot traffic, retargeting, bottom of funnel

**Visual Direction:**
Offer-forward. Price, value stack, or guarantee prominently displayed. Clean and direct.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Specific offer with specific terms]"
Body: [What's included + what it costs + guarantee]
Caption: [Why now + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Clarity + loss aversion (specific offer) + risk removal

**Example Hook:** "Free ad account audit ($500 value) for e-commerce brands spending $5K+/month on Meta."

---

### Style 32: The Limited Time Frame

**Best For:** Retargeting, existing leads, real deadlines

**Visual Direction:**
Timer or calendar visual (only for real deadlines). Urgency colors (red, orange).

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Offer] ends [specific date/time] because [genuine reason]"
Body: [What they get + what they miss if they don't act]
Caption: [Simple CTA with deadline reiterated]
```

**Psychology:** Real scarcity + loss aversion + urgency

**Example Hook:** "We're accepting 3 more clients this month. After that, we close intake until Q2."

---

### Style 33: The Free Trial or Audit

**Best For:** Skeptical prospects, high-consideration purchases

**Visual Direction:**
Value of the free offer visually represented. What they receive without risk.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Get [specific valuable thing] free. No strings."
Body: [What they'll receive + what it normally costs + why it's free]
Caption: [What happens after + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Reciprocity + risk removal + foot-in-door

**Example Hook:** "Free 20-minute ad account audit for e-commerce brands. You'll leave with 5 specific improvements regardless of whether we work together."

---

### Style 34: The Value Stack Ad

**Best For:** Consideration to decision stage

**Visual Direction:**
List of deliverables with values. Total value vs. actual price prominently shown.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Here's everything you get:"
Body: [Itemized list with values]
[Total value: $X]
[Your investment: $Y]
Caption: [Guarantee + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Anchoring + value perception + offer clarity

**Example Hook:** "Here's exactly what's included in our Growth Package:"

---

### Style 35: The Guarantee-Forward Ad

**Best For:** Risk-averse audiences, premium price points

**Visual Direction:**
Guarantee badge or statement prominently featured. Trust symbols visible.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "If we don't [specific result] in [timeframe], [specific guarantee action]"
Body: [What you get + the guarantee details]
Caption: [Why we can offer this guarantee]
CTA: [Start risk-free]
```

**Psychology:** Risk elimination + authority (we're confident) + loss aversion removal

**Example Hook:** "If we don't improve your ROAS by 25% in 90 days, we work for free until we do."

---

### Style 36: The ROI Calculator Ad

**Best For:** B2B, performance services, logical buyers

**Visual Direction:**
Simple calculator or math shown visually. Input + output = ROI clearly displayed.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Quick math:"
Body: [Current spend × our improvement = your result]
[Minus our fee = your net gain]
Caption: [These numbers are based on [real data] + CTA to verify for their situation]
```

**Psychology:** Anchoring + loss aversion + logical justification

**Example Hook:** "Quick math: $10K/month ad spend × 89% ROAS improvement = $8,900 in additional monthly revenue. Our fee: $2,997."

---

### Style 37: The Urgency Stack

**Best For:** End of consideration, decision-ready prospects

**Visual Direction:**
Multiple urgency signals combined. Not frantic but conclusive.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Three reasons to start this week, not next month:"
Body:
1. [Real urgency reason 1]
2. [Real urgency reason 2]
3. [Real cost of delay]
Caption: [CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Loss aversion × 3 + specificity + momentum

**Example Hook:** "Three reasons smart advertisers are starting Q1 now, not in January."

---

### Style 38: The Comparison Destroyer

**Best For:** Competitive markets, educated buyers

**Visual Direction:**
Your solution vs. alternatives. Honest about where you're not the right fit too.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Your solution] vs. [Alternative] vs. [Alternative 2]"
Body: [Honest comparison on dimensions that matter]
Caption: [Who each option is right for - be honest]
CTA: [If X is you, we're probably your best option]
```

**Psychology:** Honesty builds trust + authority + decision simplification

**Example Hook:** "Performance agency vs. freelancer vs. in-house: The honest breakdown for e-commerce brands."

---

### Style 39: The Retargeting Reminder

**Best For:** Retargeting audiences who visited but didn't convert

**Visual Direction:**
Familiar brand elements. Feels like a continuation, not a new introduction.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Still thinking about [thing they viewed]?"
Body: [Remove the specific objection that likely stopped them]
Caption: [What they'll lose by not acting + easy CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Mere exposure effect + objection removal + loss aversion

**Example Hook:** "Still thinking about the free audit? Here's the #1 reason people hesitate (and why it doesn't apply here)."

---

### Style 40: The Deadline Announcement

**Best For:** Real deadline situations (capacity, pricing, seasons)

**Visual Direction:**
Calendar or date prominently shown. Clear, simple, direct.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "[Specific thing] closes [specific date/when X happens]"
Body: [What happens after + why the deadline is real]
Caption: [What to do now + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Real scarcity + loss aversion + clarity

## STYLES 41-50: PATTERN INTERRUPT STYLES

**Best For:** Saturated markets needing scroll-stop

**Visual Direction:**
Looks like it doesn't belong in a feed. Could look like a text message, handwritten note, spreadsheet, or other non-ad format.

Depends on format chosen. Should feel like accidental discovery of real information.

**Psychology:** Pattern interrupt + curiosity + authenticity

**Example Hook:** A screenshot of a "text message" conversation: "Just checked our ROAS... it's 4.1x" "Wait what? Show me"

---

Metric or stat that contradicts conventional wisdom shown prominently

Strong typography, minimal visual noise. The statement IS the visual.

**Copy Structure:**

Hook: [Deliberately provocative true statement]

```

Body: [Your expert take on the trend]
Caption: [Actionable implication + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Relevance + authority + timeliness

---

### Style 46: The Visual Paradox

**Best For:** Highly visual platforms, scroll-heavy environments

**Visual Direction:**
Two things that don't belong together or a visual that requires explanation. Forces the viewer to stop and figure it out.

**Psychology:** Pattern interrupt + curiosity + engagement

**Example:** Image of a fire extinguisher with "For your ad account" or money in a blender with "What bad creative does to your budget"

---

### Style 47: The Text Dump

**Best For:** High-intent cold audiences, LinkedIn, long-form platforms

**Visual Direction:**
Wall of text that starts with something compelling enough to make reading feel worthwhile. Intentionally "undesigned".

**Copy Structure:**
```
Opening line: [Compelling hook - first line must earn the second]
Paragraph 2: [Problem specificity]
Paragraph 3: [Solution mechanism]
Paragraph 4: [Proof]
Final line: [CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Authority (requires confidence) + specificity + reciprocity (value delivered in the ad)

**Example Hook:** "I'm going to tell you exactly why your Meta ROAS drops every time you scale, and what to do about it. No opt-in, no catch. Just the answer. [long form follows]"

---

Looks like it was just captured. Phone screenshot, live video thumbnail, "just happened" energy.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "Just got this from a client..."
Body: [The result/message/update]
Caption: [Context + what this means for the viewer]
CTA: [Want the same?]
```

**Psychology:** Social proof + recency + authenticity

**Example Hook:** "Just got this from a client we onboarded 87 days ago: [screenshot of their results message]"

---

### Style 49: The Reversal

**Best For:** Breaking through objection-hardened audiences

**Visual Direction:**
Expectation set-up then subverted. Viewer expects one thing, gets another.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Set-up: [What they expect you to say]
Reverse: "Actually, [the opposite or unexpected truth]"
Explanation: [Why the reversal is true]
CTA: [The implication for their situation]
```

**Psychology:** Confirmation bias disruption + curiosity + trust (honesty signals)

**Example Hook:** "Most agencies will tell you to increase your budget to improve results. We usually tell our new clients the opposite."

### Style 50: The Invitation

**Best For:** Community building, event promotion, relationship-first brands

**Visual Direction:**
Warm, inclusive imagery. Feels like being welcomed into something valuable.

**Copy Structure:**
```
Hook: "You're invited to [specific thing]."
Body: [What it is + what they'll get + who it's for]
Caption: [Why this matters to them specifically + CTA]
```

**Psychology:** Exclusivity + belonging + IKEA effect (investment in attending)

**Example Hook:** "You're invited to a free live workshop: How we build creative testing systems for 7-figure e-commerce brands."
